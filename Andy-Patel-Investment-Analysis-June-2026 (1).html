Executive Summary

Five vacant townhome and condo investment properties between $333K and $350K, ranked for an all-cash buyer on the factors we can stand behind today. With rent set aside until your property manager confirms it, the deal comes down to four things: how motivated the seller is, how cheap the property is to hold, how new it is, and how strong the address is for long-term tenant demand and appreciation.

The Trade-Off in This Set

There's a clean tension across these five worth naming up front: you can buy new construction, or you can buy a true Lake Nona address — but not both. The two newest builds (Narcoossee Commons and Cyrils, both 2023) sit in St. Cloud / Sunbridge, not Lake Nona. The genuine Lake Nona addresses (Eagle Creek and East Park) are older product. The four levers below are how the ranking weighs that trade-off.

Important Disclosures

  • Rent is excluded by design. Historical lease figures on these units are stale; no income, yield, or cash-on-cash is modeled. A current rental analysis by your property manager is required before any offer and will determine the actual return.
  • All-cash assumption. No mortgage modeled. Holding cost is therefore your full monthly cost of ownership until rent is established.
  • Holding cost = property tax (most recent county bill) + HOA + CDD + estimated owner insurance. Excludes management, vacancy, capex reserves, and any future special assessments.
  • Property tax. Narcoossee Commons currently carries a homestead exemption that will reset upward on sale to a non-owner-occupant; its holding cost uses a normalized estimate.
  • Seller purchase prices from Orange/Osceola county and comparable records: Silver Buttonwood ~$169,900 (2015), Lake District $195,000 (2016), Narcoossee Commons $351,520 (2023). Cyrils and Millington were builder-direct/off-MLS; figures are inferred from identical-model comps.
  • Condo note. Lake District #55B is the only condominium; confirm reserve funding, milestone/SIRS status, and special-assessment history before close. The listing states it is not currently subject to milestone inspection or SIRS.

Recommended Next Steps

Complete a current rental analysis before choosing

This is the gating step. Verandah pulls live rental comps within 0.5–1 mile of your shortlisted properties to establish achievable rent today. Paired with the holding costs in this report, that confirmed number is what turns a "good deal" into a quantified return — and it may reorder these five.

Decide your priority: carry, newness, or address

The set forces a choice. If lowest cost-to-hold wins, it's Narcoossee Commons. If a true Lake Nona address and appreciation win, it's Millington (Eagle Creek). If best motivation-plus-location balance wins, it's Silver Buttonwood — the current top pick.

Lead with Silver Buttonwood at $315K cash

Pending the rental analysis, this is the best risk-adjusted entry: deepest equity cushion behind the seller, longest time on market, lightest carry of the Lake Nona addresses. Full proof of funds, fast close, condition contingency given the 347 days.

Front-load diligence on the older and condo units

Silver Buttonwood (2013, long DOM) and Lake District (2008 condo) both warrant early inspection. For Lake District, request the condo docs, reserve study, and special-assessment history before going hard on deposit.
